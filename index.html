Proof That Closes Deals

Two stories from Stoke Space. Both real. Both verifiable.

The Washer Incident

Problem: 10,000 out-of-spec washers discovered in supply chain.

Without Boltline: Weeks of manual archaeology. Spreadsheets, emails, tribal knowledge. Maybe you find them all, maybe one detonates during a test.

With Boltline: Queried the system. Every shelf location, every affected assembly. 4 washers inside an active engine. Reworked and hot fired the same day.

2 hours vs. 2 weeks. Every hardware founder has lived a version of this. This story IS the pitch.

The Bottleneck Discovery

Problem: Welded barrel assembly too slow. Obvious fix: more welding stations. Cost: $200K+.

Without Boltline: Capital expenditure based on gut feel.

With Boltline: Mapped work plan in detail. Bottleneck was inspection sequencing, not welding. Moved checks earlier, cut redundant sign-offs. Cycle time dropped without spending a dollar.

The tool pays for itself by preventing the wrong capital expenditure. Hardware founders understand this math instantly.
Honest gap: These are Boltline’s own stories — vendor proof, not customer proof. The first SpaceX alumni customer who can tell THEIR version of this story unlocks the entire segment. That’s why landing pilot #1 is the real objective.

Scoring rubric (100 pts)

  • Leadership fit (25): 25 = founder/CTO ex-SpaceX. 15 = VP-level. 5 = engineer-level, no decision authority
  • Company fit (25): 25 = complex HW, 50–200 headcount, production ramp. 15 = prototype stage. 5 = pre-product

  • Pain likelihood (20): 20 = known compliance/traceability pressure. 10 = inferred. 0 = no evidence

Honest Funnel Math

Conservative numbers, not aspirational ones

For every 100 people we email, only a handful reply. Of those, fewer take a meeting. Even fewer do a deep-dive. And only a fraction of those sign a pilot. The math below shows exactly how many pilots we can expect from each path over 90 days.

With warm intro (Stoke network connection)

  • Emails/month: 3–5 (limited by warm connections available)
  • Positive reply rate: 25–40% (warm intro = not cold)
  • Reply → meeting: 60%
  • Meeting → diagnostic: 50%
  • Diagnostic → pilot: 30%

90-day yield (15 warm touches): 15 × .30 × .60 × .50 × .30 = ~0.4 pilots

Without warm intro (trigger-based cold)

  • Emails/month: 8–10 Tier A + 15–20 Tier B
  • Positive reply rate: 8–12% Tier A, 4–6% Tier B (industry: 3–5% cold, we’re above because trigger-based + shared context)
  • Reply → meeting: 40%
  • Meeting → diagnostic: 50%
  • Diagnostic → pilot: 25%

90-day yield (30 Tier A + 60 Tier B):
Tier A: 30 × .10 × .40 × .50 × .25 = ~0.15 pilots
Tier B: 60 × .05 × .40 × .50 × .25 = ~0.15 pilots

Combined 90-day forecast: ~0.7 pilots from math alone. That rounds to 1 — maybe 2 if warm intros outperform. Not 3–5. The honest target is 1–2 signed pilots in 90 days. That’s still a win — the first customer unlocks peer proof, referrals, and case study rights. Overpromising kills credibility.
Day-1 Operational Stack

What’s already running — and what to add

Most of the infrastructure is already live. This campaign layers on top of existing tooling, not a greenfield build.

Already live (no setup needed)

  • HubSpot CRM: Already integrated via Leadin plugin. Add custom properties: tier, score, trigger, objection, next action. AI-assisted lead scoring on ingest.
  • Reb2b intent data: Already tracking B2B visitors. Use to detect when target accounts hit boltline.com — instant Tier A promotion trigger.
  • Datadog RUM: Already monitoring at 20% sample rate. Track LP1/LP2 engagement, time-on-page, and scroll depth for campaign pages.
  • Google Ads: Conversion tracking live (AW-11150013242). Use for retargeting LP1 visitors who don’t book.
  • WordPress + Cloudflare: LP1 and LP2 ship as WordPress pages behind existing CDN. No new infra needed.

Configure (Week -2 to 0)

  • Email domain: Set up outreach@boltline.com with SPF, DKIM, DMARC. 2–3 weeks warm-up via Instantly or Lemlist before volume sends.
  • HubSpot sequences: Build 21-day outreach cadence as HubSpot sequence. Auto-log touches, replies, and status changes.
  • Reb2b → HubSpot: Connect Reb2b to HubSpot workflows. When a Tier A/B account visits boltline.com, auto-create task for Marketing.
  • Tracking rules: Pixel tracking OFF for Tier A emails — technical founders block/notice it. Track replies only. Use Reb2b for site-side intent.

Add (new tools)

  • LinkedIn SN: $100/mo. Saved search for SpaceX alumni + title + company size filters. Weekly digest into HubSpot.
  • Crunchbase Pro: $49/mo. Follow all Tier A accounts. Funding round = instant trigger alert.
  • Google Alerts: Free. “[company name]” + funding / facility / production / SBIR for each Tier A.
  • SAM.gov: Free. Bookmark searches for target company names. Check weekly.

New tool cost: $149/mo. Config time: ~4 hours.

Key insight: The hard infrastructure is already in place. This campaign is a configuration exercise — custom properties in HubSpot, a Reb2b workflow, an email warm-up, and two WordPress landing pages. No new platforms to procure or integrate.

What Kills This Motion

Five failure modes, each with a specific prevention

1. No warm intros available

If the Stoke team has zero 1st-degree connections to these founders, the warm path dries up and you’re fully cold. Cold-only reply rates drop to 5–8%. Fix: Map connections in Week 2. If fewer than 5 warm paths exist, prioritize conference dinners (Space Symposium) to build them.

2. “SpaceX alumni” becomes the pitch

If outreach says “Hey fellow SpaceX person!” instead of addressing operational pain, it’s dead. Fix: SpaceX background is the targeting criterion, never the message. The message is always about their current scaling pain.

3. Volume temptation

“Why 10/month? Let’s blast 200.” Then reply rates crash and the network is permanently burned. Fix: Hard cap. 10 Tier A, 20 Tier B. These people talk. One bad mass email poisons the entire segment.

4. Meetings die after “cool demo”

No structured next step after diagnostic. Fix: Every diagnostic ends with scoped pilot proposal: one product line, 30–60 days, 3 measurable outcomes. If they can’t commit, they’re not ready — move to nurture.

5. Marketing is the bottleneck. All Tier A runs through them. If also running product, capacity is 3–5 hrs/week max. Fix: Growth hire owns all monitoring, enrichment, scoring, and Tier B. Marketing only writes Tier A emails (2–3/week) and runs diagnostic calls. By Month 3, SE can run overflow calls with Marketing’s talk track.
6 Numbers + Weekly Review

Track these. Ignore everything else.

1

Trigger signals / week

Target: 3–8. Zero for 2 weeks = monitoring is broken or target list is too narrow.

2

Positive reply rate

Warm: 25–40%. Cold Tier A: 8–12%. Cold Tier B: 4–6%. Below these = messaging problem. Iterate copy.

3

Meetings booked / month

Target: 2–4 qualified. “Qualified” = decision-maker at Tier A/B, not a manager doing research.

4

Meeting → diagnostic

Target: 50%. Below 30% = demo isn’t connecting to their pain. Revisit first 10 min of the call.

5

Pipeline ($)

Cumulative dollar value of qualified opportunities from this segment. The number that justifies Month 4 investment.

6

Top objection theme

Not a number — a pattern. “Already use FR” vs “Not ready” vs “Too expensive” tells you completely different things. Review weekly.

Never measure: Emails sent. Open rates. LinkedIn impressions. “Touches per account.” Activity metrics make you feel productive while pipeline stays empty.
